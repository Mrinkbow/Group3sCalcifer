Order of Operations

Order of operations is well, the order of operations in math. You do parentheses first, exponents next, then multiply or divide left to right, and finally add or subtract left to right. Easily remembered as PEMDAS.

Algebra

Algebra is a branch of mathematics using symbols, generally letters, that can be replaced by a number or expressions to generalize arithmatic operations and relationships. Expressions, equations, inequalities, and integers are only some of the parts of algebra.

Angles

An angle is the space between two intersecting lines measured in degrees at or close to the point they meet. There are different types of angles, acute, right, obtuse, straight, and reflex. Each type of angle has a condition to be considered that type of angle, acute has to have degrees less than 90, right is exactly 90, obtuse is more than 90, straight is exactly 180, reflex is more than 180.

Triangles

Triangles are a basic shape of three sides. They also have the interior 180 degrees, they are a very stable shape due to the fact if you pull on one side the other two sides resist.
